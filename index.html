Build Branches

We will build All Branches at their point of need because it is the role of the State Conference to assist branches because if Branches are strong the State Conference will be stronger. We will also make it a priority to guide Branches in the areas of membership development, fundraising and program implementation. We will meet each Branch, evaluate their needs and assist and guide so each Branch can be prepared to always be in compliance with the National office and serve in their jurisdiction. We will also assign cluster coordinators for each of the regions in the state of Maryland. The State Conference will lead but we will all grow in the areas of compliance/capacity, membership, economic development, relevant statewide programs, community partnerships, election year protocol, identifying leaders and always have a significant political agenda.

Invest In Youth

Youth Council & ACT-SO

Youth Council & ACT-SO We will advance the future of the State Conference when we make youth investment a priority. That means helping all branches to assess their capacity to start Youth Councils and ACT-SO programs, strengthen existing units/programs and financially support Branches based on their needs.

Young Professionals and College division

We will identify young professionals and give them a seat at the table and each Branch will be charged with doing so. We will also identify young leaders at colleges and universities in the state. We will plan to implement manageable goals so NAACP campus chapters are a reliable source for leadership development and grooming future leaders for the future.

Lead Maryland

We will unify the Maryland State Conference. We will never be a successful Conference if we are not unified. The standard of the state conference will be collaboration and respect. We will continue to build partnerships with our traditional allies and will identify new community stakeholders. We will expand our state executive committee to include STEM/Innovation, Historically Black Colleges and Universities, Mentoring, Advocacy for Sexual Minorities, Latino/Hispanic Outreach and Suburban Solutions. We will also establish a leadership development structure which will attract, train and launch political leaders for the state of Maryland. We will also transition from a volunteer service organization to one that hires full and part-time staff to do the work of the NAACP in the state.
